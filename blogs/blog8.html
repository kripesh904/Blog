Nightmare in Reality

Kripesh Raj Sharma

After a long and tiring day at work,
A lady walks down the road of street 14.
Hating every step of the walk in her high heels,
Listening to her favorite Taylor Swift,
Carrying her favourite blue bag.
Her house in street 17 seems like a prolonged dream,
Never seeing the day of light.

The light of the sun has sunk and now,
The day has darkened itself.
She is still violently cursing her heels,
And the sun, for setting this early.
When she reaches street 15,
Gasping on the achievement,
She stops to fix her heel and
Takes the airpods off her ear,
Finally able to hear.

Right then,
A man enters the scene.
A man.
The words spoken by him when
She was still fixing the heel made her wish
The airpods were still in her ears.
She fearing of the man's intentions,
carrying the heels in one hand,
and the bag and pods in another,
briskly walked.
When he started following her steps,
she hurriedly runs.
Runs from the silent alley of the street.
But, the man was faster.
He chased her down.
Pushed her in the wall with his hands,
The red heels banged the ground,
The bag struck the dirt.
Airpods broke.

He, grippingly catched her by her waist,
pinning her to the wall.
The girl was still fighting and screaming
for her life.
But he made her his only quest,
Now, all heard was cries and screams,
The drunk bastard with his lustful schemes,
He, starts to scratch her chest,
The woman, still numb, the man starts to undress.
The teenager trying to lose the grip,
bites his forearm, on the vein,
slowly the grip losens and she finally
throws the man away.
She runs and runs and runs,
and runs and runs and runs,
as if it was the last run of her life.

She reached street 17,
faster than the whole journey.
She rushingly approaches her mother,
with dirt on the tore clothes,
naked feet,
and eyes full of tears.
She tried to utter words,
but the words only meant to self-loathe.
Her mother asks and asks but
She didn't utter a single moan,
Why? Because she was all alone.
Her mother was undoubtedly with her but
she also would have asked her to forget it
For in our society,
Itâ€™s better to live with scars all life with secrets engraved,
Rather than living enslaved, with remarks and stamps.

The reasons why the words are left unsaid and unfinished,
The words which made her inner self rot her esteem,
The words which filled her whole body with disgust.
Swallowing every bit of tear and hurt,
She continued to live her life.
But, she never wore heels ever again.
Never bought anything blue,
Never listened to Taylor Swift,
Never approached the 15th street,
Never raised her head,
Never faced herself in a mirror,
Never truly lived.
Because something died that night.
